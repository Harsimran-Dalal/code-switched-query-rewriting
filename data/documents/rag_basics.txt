RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) basics

RAG systems answer questions by retrieving relevant documents/passages and then generating an answer grounded in those passages.

Typical steps:
1) Embed the query and documents into vectors (dense retrieval) or use lexical matching (BM25).
2) Retrieve top-k relevant chunks.
3) Provide the retrieved context to a generator to produce the final answer.

Benefits:
- Improves factual grounding (less hallucination)
- Enables domain-specific answers without training a new model
